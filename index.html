The idea

Future hands you one.

Endless choice isn't romance — it's a slot machine. So we took the feed out. There's no grid to thumb through, and no queue behind the person you're looking at.

Future picks a single person for you each day, based on a real conversation about who you are — not on how fast your thumb moves.

The app

One card.
One clock.

This is the whole app. Not a stack, not a deck — a single card that arrives each day and quietly runs out of time.

  • RingTwelve hours, counting down for both of you at once. When it empties, the match is gone.
  • AcceptSay yes. If they said yes too, the chat opens — with a plan for the night already in it.
  • PassSay no and the day is done. Tomorrow brings someone new. Nothing to scroll in between.

How it works

Three steps.
Then patience.

01

A conversation

Not a form with sliders. Future talks to you, and learns what actually matters — the things you'd never think to put in a bio.

02

One person

Two people a day, one at a time. Chosen for you, and you for them. Nobody is queued behind them, so there's nothing to compare against.

03

Twelve hours

You both have twelve hours to say yes. If you both do, the chat opens. If not, tomorrow brings someone new.

A day is long enough to be sure.

No pressure to swipe before you've thought about it. No ghosting into a pile of other faces. One person, one window, one decision.

When it clicks

the night.

Say yes to each other and the chat opens with a plan already in it — three places to eat and three things to do, near where you both are. You each see the same list, so nobody has to be the one who decides.
